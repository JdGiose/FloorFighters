About Floor Fighter

It started on the floor. It still lives there.

Built by dancers, for dancers who want their progress to mean something.

Why This Exists

Breaking has always run on respect that's earned, not given. Cyphers, sessions, battles — that's where reputations actually get built, move by move, year by year.

Floor Fighter takes that same logic and gives it structure: a system of Gyms, Gym Leaders, and real challenges — so a medal means you actually went through something, not just signed up for it.

No shortcuts. No instant access. You train, you prove it, you battle.

What We're Building

A Real Path

Not a one-off tournament. A progression — from your first Toprock to standing across from a Gym Leader who's already walked the same road.

Leaders Who've Earned It

Every Gym Leader trained through the full program before they got the right to host battles and judge fighters. Nobody skips the line.

A System, Not a Gimmick

Move sets, eligibility checks, verified battles, recorded results. The structure exists so your work actually counts for something.

Who It's For

The Self-Motivated

Dancers who train without being told to, and want their progress to mean something outside their own head.

The Unconvinced

The ones who've watched the scene get diluted by hype and easy access, and still want something that asks something of you.

— THE POINT OF ALL THIS —

Strip away the gyms, the medals, the rules — what's left is the same thing that's always made this culture worth being part of: people showing up for each other, pushing each other, building something real together on the floor.

That's the whole reason Floor Fighter exists.
